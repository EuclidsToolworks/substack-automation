Euclid's Toolworks—"Woke Up This Morning With A Wine Glass In My Hand..."

So what's with this crazy title, huh? It's been a minute since my last post, in June. I wouldn't be surprised if all of Euclid's subscribers have left the building. Some of you out there may recognize the opening line from Peter Frampton's Do You Feel Like We Do. Here's the whole first stanza:

Do You Feel Like We Do1

Woke up this morning with a wine glass in my hand
Whose wine? What wine?
Where the hell did I dine?
Must have been a dream
I don't believe where I've been
Come on, let's do it again

Everything pretty much describes life since this past April when I went back to work, except I don't want to do it again. I was planning on working until my youngest son graduated from Engineering School in May of 2026, but my health problems started getting worse again when I started back at the desk all day, and then some. I decided to chuck it and get the shop going a few years early.

In short, I'm back at it, only this time it's for good. It will take a lot of work before I can start producing products for sale, but as of December 19th, 2025, I've got all day to get things going.

Below is a short summary of updates to the substack that are going out alongside this post.

  1. There's somewhere around a half dozen new books in the library.
  2. A new project has been added to project page, so expect a series of posts on putting a traditional finish on my new pine window sills.

So to summarize, Euclid's Toolworks is open for business, finally after more than a year. I'll have a lot more time to write for all y'all so feel free to send in ideas. I've got more than a few topics on the way.

So let's turn it up and Laissez les bons temps rouler!

  1. Peter Frampton, "Do You Feel Like We Do," on Frampton's Camel, A&M Records, 1973. ↩
